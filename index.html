Forex Basics & Education

Whether you're new to Forex or looking to brush up on fundamentals, our educational section covers:

What is Forex?

The Foreign Exchange (Forex) market is where currencies are traded globally. The purpose can be speculation, hedging, or simply exchanging for business/travel.

Visit Bloomberg Currencies for live updates.

Check out Investing.com for FX analysis.

Risk Disclosure

Forex trading involves substantial risk. Always understand margin calls, leverage limitations, and be prepared for market volatility. We recommend practicing on a demo account before live trading.

Short vs. Long Positions

Long position: Buying a currency pair in anticipation that its value will rise.
Short position: Selling a currency pair believing its value will drop.

Lots & Leverage

A lot represents a standardized unit of currency. Leverage (e.g., 1:100) amplifies your buying power but also magnifies risks.

Understanding Rebates

Forex rebates let you receive a portion of the spread or commission back on your trades. This lowers overall trading costs and can be an additional earning opportunity.

Banner Placeholder #2 (300x250)

How Rebates Work

1. You register with a broker through an affiliate or IB link.
2. You trade Forex pairs with the broker.
3. The broker pays a portion of the spread or commission back to the affiliate, who then shares it with you.

Since each broker offers different rates, it’s important to choose one that fits your trading style (scalping, day trading, swing trading, etc.).
